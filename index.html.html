Kedah-Kelantan Cattle Growth Simulator

Applying Calculus & Derivatives to Malaysian Animal Husbandry Technology

SFH: Animal Husbandry Technology

The Bioscience Problem

In Malaysian beef cattle production, Kedah-Kelantan (KK) cattle represent the cornerstone of indigenous genetics. They are highly adapted to hot, humid tropical climates and feed of poorer nutritional quality.

A major challenge for animal husbandry scientists is determining the **optimal slaughter age** or identifying when an animal's feed utilization is at peak efficiency.

By modeling their growth $W(t)$ and calculating its first derivative $W'(t)$ (representing weight gain rate, or Average Daily Gain - ADG), we locate the inflection point. This point marks the transition from accelerating growth to decelerating growth, allowing farmers to maximize profitability and feed conversion efficiency.

Mathematical Modeling

Gompertz Growth Curve $f(t)$:
$$W(t) = A \cdot e^{-B \cdot e^{-C \cdot t}}$$
First Derivative $f'(t)$ (Growth Rate):
$$W'(t) = A \cdot B \cdot C \cdot e^{-C \cdot t} \cdot e^{-B \cdot e^{-C \cdot t}}$$

Where:

$t$: Age of cattle in months.

$W(t)$: Live weight in kilograms (kg).

$W'(t)$: Instantaneous growth rate (kg/month).

$A$: Asymptotic mature weight (genetic limit).

$B$: Initial growth scaling parameter.

$C$: Maturing growth rate index (feed quality indicator).
